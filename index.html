Against the Wind

A sailboat can sail toward the wind. Drag the wind around, watch the forces on the sail and the keel, and see the no-go zone appear. Then switch to a Napoleonic square-rigger and watch it yawn open to six points — a limit the simulation is never told, and works out for itself.

How these are made

Every episode runs the same six-stage pipeline, and the part worth stealing is the division of labour: a fleet of research agents verifies the physics before a line of code is written, each working under a standing instruction to write UNVERIFIED rather than guess — and afterwards a browser agent drives the finished page through QA. The fleet checks, tests and publishes. It never originates content. That is the whole anti-slop gate.

It bites, too. Building Episode 1, the research fleet flatly refused to invent a polar diagram it could not source. That cost a calibration target and left the page more honest than it would otherwise have been. The playbook is written down in WORKSHOP.md.
